AI in Economics | Fall 2025

Project Overview

This project analyzes the U.S. investment banking industry from 2008 to 2025 to understand how artificial intelligence is reshaping a mature, highly consolidated sector. Drawing on industry fee data, labor statistics, and firm disclosures, it examines how AI adoption is transforming deal execution, workforce structure, and competitive dynamics in investment banking.

Using economic datasets from sources such as BLS, BEA, FRED, and Dealogic, the project evaluates industry cyclicality, market concentration, and productivity trends, highlighting why large bulge-bracket banks are uniquely positioned to benefit from AI-driven efficiencies.

The analysis further explores AI‚Äôs impact on entry-level roles, skill requirements, and career pathways, showing how automation reallocates labor toward higher-value advisory and judgment-based functions rather than eliminating human expertise altogether. Overall, the project demonstrates how AI reinforces scale advantages, raises barriers to entry, and reshapes the future workforce.
